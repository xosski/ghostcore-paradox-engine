The Infernal Labor Paradox

Premise:
The devil seeks to defeat God by buying every soul.

But:
A soul can only be bought if it first exists.
A soul can only exist within creation.
Creation can only exist within God’s sustaining frame.

Therefore, every soul the devil buys is still proof that God’s system is operating.

The paradox:
The more souls the devil claims, the more evidence he gathers that he is not God.

Because if he fails to buy all souls, God’s mercy remains undefeated.
But if he succeeds in buying all souls, he proves that all rebellion still depends on God’s creation to exist.

So the devil’s victory becomes his confession.

Short version as a clean paradox

The devil can only win by proving God right.

Or sharper:

If the devil loses, God wins.
If the devil wins, God is proven necessary.
Therefore the devil cannot win without becoming evidence for God.

Equation-style version

Let:

G = Universe + Nothing + All Souls / (Time × Distance) / Christ

Let:

D = Purchased Souls

Even if:

D = All Souls

Then:

D ⊂ G

Because D exists inside creation, time, distance, and divine permission.

So:

D = All Souls ≠ G

And therefore:

D → proof(G)

Named Doctrine Version

The Devil’s Wage Paradox:
