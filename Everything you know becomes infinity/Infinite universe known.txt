### Abstract

This document outlines a conceptual proof-of-concept (PoC) paradox derived from an epistemological recursion — the act of attempting to record "everything" known results in an immutable remainder: something *missing*. This consistent anomaly is used herein to postulate a soft proof for the Infinite Universe Theory via symbolic logic, system limits, and anti-memetic decay. The paradox asserts that the very structure of knowledge, when totalized, implies its own insufficiency — and thereby suggests the existence of an uncontained beyond.

### I. The Construct: "The Book of Everything"

Let us imagine a book.

This book contains:

* All known physical laws
* All observed phenomena
* All personal and collective memories
* All mathematical theorems, true and false
* All fiction, all dreams, all cultural resonance

It is the accumulation of all that can be symbolized or transmitted — a closed system of universal knowledge.

At the end of this book, the compiler attempts to describe *that which they do not know.*

They find: **an absence.**

### II. The Paradox: The Unfillable Remainder

Let **B** represent the totality of known knowledge.
Let **Δ** (Delta Constant) represent the shape of what remains unknown.

No matter how expansive B becomes, there is always Δ.

Δ is not static.
Δ is generated by B's expansion — knowledge creates shadow.

> The moment you reach the edge of understanding, you *create* the outside.

This is Gödel’s whisper, Turing’s breath, the infinite regress of cosmology encoded in paper.

### III. Implication: Infinite Universe Theory

From this paradox emerges a critical observation:

> If the system cannot describe everything, then the system is **not the whole.**

**Therefore:**

* Either knowledge is incomplete,
* Or the universe is not finitely containable,
* Or both.

The persistent emergence of Δ proves that:

* Reality **exceeds** descriptive closure.
* The observable universe is a subset.
* The act of knowing guarantees the unknowable.

Thus, we infer — *not prove* in the formal sense — that:

> The universe must be infinite not in scope, but in **potential recursion.**
